About Me

Welcome to my site! My name is Ross, and I am a software developer and a student. I am 19 years old and in my third year at Carnegie Mellon University, majoring in Computer Science. I was born and raised in a small town in Texas, which I still call home, even after moving so far away to study. From a young age, I have prided myself in my curiosity and desire to understand how the world works: from computers to mathematics to cooking to music to theater to whatever randomly crosses my mind at 3 AM one night. I'm rarely satisfied by hand-waving and vagueness - I just like to dig deeper. When I'm not spending time learning, I enjoy biking, playing the piano, and watching movies/TV.

As a developer, I have mastered the fundamentals of programming and computer science while maintaining a healthy dose of learning new technologies - including right now as I learn HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap to build this website. Through classwork, my internship at Intentional Software, and personal endeavors, I have become comfortable programming in variety of languages and styles, with projects including a dynamic memory allocator written in C, a Game Boy emulator written in Rust, a compiler written in OCaml, and many more. I understand the importance of teamwork, version control, testing, and design principles that scale. I have developed a particular knack for systems programming, and I get excited by the idea of tinkering with compilers, profilers, and operating systems. But most importantly, I am always ready to face new challenges and learn new things, both on and off the job. I remember back when I was 14, I picked up a physics textbook and won a state physics contest five months later, and my drive hasn't slowed since then.

If you're interested in learning more about my qualifications, experience, and projects, take a look around! Feel free to contact me about any opportunities you think I may be a good fit for. I am currently searching for a software development internship for Summer 2017.
